Box Model Practice

This is a box model example. The content area is 200px wide and 100px tall. The padding adds 20px of space around the content, the border adds 5px of thickness, and the margin creates 10px of space outside the border.

The box model consists of four main components: content, padding, border, and margin. The content is the innermost part of the box where text or images are placed. The padding is the space between the content and the border, which can be adjusted to create more space around the content. The border is a line that surrounds the padding and content, which can be styled with different colors and widths. Finally, the margin is the outermost layer that creates space between the box and other elements on the page. Understanding the box model is essential for designing and laying out web pages effectively.

By mastering the box model, you can control the layout and spacing of elements on your web page, ensuring that your design looks exactly how you want it to.
